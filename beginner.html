FOUNDATION SERIES

BEGINNER SKILLS

Everything you need to go from never touched a wakeboard to confidently riding both directions across the wake.

Module 1 – Getting On the Water

Before you ever edge the wake, you need to get comfortable with your gear and your first ride up. This module sets the foundation.

Level 1

Choosing Your Stance

Regular (left foot forward) or goofy (right foot forward)? Most people discover their natural stance instinctively. Here's how to find yours.

  • ✓ Slide test: have someone push you gently from behind. The foot you put forward is your lead foot.
  • ✓ Set bindings shoulder-width apart at a 15–18° duck stance for most beginners.
  • ✓ Knees slightly bent over toes — never lock your knees.
Level 1

Deep-Water Start

The deep-water start is the single hardest skill for beginners. Patience and the right body position make all the difference.

  • ✓ Float on your back, knees drawn to chest, board tips up out of the water.
  • ✓ Arms straight, let the rope pull you — resist the urge to stand up early.
  • ✓ Only stand when the board is fully planing and you're on top of the water.
  • ✓ Rope between your knees keeps the board on track during the pull.
Level 1

Riding Position

Great riding posture prevents fatigue and keeps you balanced when waves or boat wake hit unexpectedly.

  • ✓ Weight centered, slightly on your back foot to keep nose up.
  • ✓ Hips square over the board, not twisting toward the boat.
  • ✓ Handle at your hip — elbows slightly bent, arms relaxed.
  • ✓ Eyes forward on the horizon, not down at your feet.
Level 1

Falling Safely

You will fall — a lot. Knowing how to fall correctly prevents injuries and builds your confidence to try harder things.

  • ✓ Fall sideways, never forward onto your face.
  • ✓ Cross arms over your chest when falling backward to protect your hands.
  • ✓ Go limp — tensing up causes injuries.
  • ✓ Wait until the boat circles back; hold the board up so the driver can see you.

Module 2 – Edge Control & Direction

Edge control is the core skill of wakeboarding. Once you can edge confidently, every other skill becomes accessible.

Level 2

Heelside & Toeside Edges

Shift your weight to your heels to cut toward your heelside, toes to cut toeside. It's subtle — you don't need to lean dramatically.

  • ✓ Practice slow, controlled side-to-side traverses before crossing the wake.
  • ✓ Keep your weight over the board — don't lean against the rope.
  • ✓ The handle stays low at your hip during edge cuts.
Level 2

Crossing the Wake

Your first wake crossing is a milestone. Build up speed gradually by cutting wider from the boat before crossing.

  • ✓ Start slow: ride flat across the small inside wake first.
  • ✓ Keep knees bent to absorb the pop at the wake lip.
  • ✓ Land with weight centered; don't throw your weight back on landing.
  • ✓ Progress to stronger cuts and larger wake crossings over multiple sessions.
Level 2

180 Surface Spin

Your first "trick" — a 180 surface rotation that teaches handle passes and board control simultaneously.

  • ✓ Ride flat on the water before initiating.
  • ✓ Pull the handle to your back hip and rotate your hips 180°.
  • ✓ You'll end up riding switch (opposite stance). Practice riding switch before attempting.
Level 2

Switch Riding

Riding in your unnatural stance is uncomfortable at first but opens up a huge range of tricks once mastered.

  • ✓ Ask the boat to slow down 2–3 mph for your first switch runs.
  • ✓ Focus on balance — everything feels backwards but the principles are identical.
  • ✓ Practice switch deep-water starts once comfortable riding switch.
