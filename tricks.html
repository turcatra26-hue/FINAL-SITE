PROGRESSION SYSTEM

TRICK PROGRESSION

A structured path from surface tricks and small jumps all the way to inverts and blind landings. Work the levels in order.

Phase 1 – Surface Tricks

Master these before leaving the water. They train your handle mechanics and board feel that every aerial trick depends on.

Beginner

Ollie

The foundation of all wake jumps. Practice the ollie on flat water before taking it to the wake.

  • ✓ Load your back foot, then snap upward with both feet simultaneously.
  • ✓ Bring knees up toward your chest at the peak.
  • ✓ Spot your landing and extend legs to absorb on touchdown.
Beginner

Surface 180 / 360

Spinning on the water builds the rotational awareness that aerial spins require.

  • ✓ 180: pull handle to back hip, rotate; land riding switch.
  • ✓ 360: continue the rotation, pass the handle behind your back.
  • ✓ Keep the handle close to your body throughout the rotation.
Beginner

Butter Slides

Nose and tail slides teach board manipulation and weight distribution in a forgiving environment.

  • ✓ Nose slide: weight over front foot, lift tail slightly.
  • ✓ Tail slide: weight over back foot, lift nose off the water.
  • ✓ Progress to linking nose → tail → nose in one smooth motion.

Phase 2 – Wake Jumps

Taking the ollie to the wake unlocks your first real air. Consistency here before adding any spins or grabs.

Intermediate

Wake Jump (Heelside)

Your first wake-to-wake jump. More about edge and patience than strength.

  • ✓ Progressive edge — start slow, build speed into the wake.
  • ✓ Maintain edge all the way to the wake lip; don't flatten off early.
  • ✓ Stand tall at the lip — let the wake pop you, don't jump.
  • ✓ Absorb landing with bent knees, weight centered.
Intermediate

Tantrum (Beginner Invert)

A back flip off the heelside wake. Surprisingly approachable because the rotation is natural with the wake's shape.

  • ✓ Requires consistent wake-to-wake jumps first.
  • ✓ Edge hard heelside, throw your head back at the lip.
  • ✓ Spot the water, bring handle to your hip, stomp landing.
Intermediate

Heelside 180

Half rotation off the heelside wake. Land riding switch across the wake — a key milestone trick.

  • ✓ Same edge approach as a clean wake jump.
  • ✓ At the lip, rotate your shoulders and pull the handle to your lead hip.
  • ✓ Land switch, ride away clean before attempting the 360.
Intermediate

Toeside Wake Jump

The toeside edge is harder for most riders. Master it because advanced tricks demand a strong toeside approach.

  • ✓ Many riders rush the toeside edge — stay patient, build gradually.
  • ✓ Hip alignment is critical: keep hips square into the wake.
  • ✓ Pop is slightly different — commit to standing tall at the lip.

Phase 3 – Advanced Spins & Inverts

For riders with solid Phase 2 fundamentals and comfortable landing switch. These tricks require full commitment.

Advanced

Heelside 360

The benchmark trick for intermediate-to-advanced progression. A full rotation with a handle pass behind the back.

  • ✓ Must have consistent heelside 180 first.
  • ✓ Pass the handle early — before you've completed 270° of rotation.
  • ✓ Spot the water through the pass and stomp with confidence.
Advanced

Backroll

A toeside invert — cleaner mechanically than the tantrum for many riders.

  • ✓ Strong toeside edge with full commitment at the wake lip.
  • ✓ Look over your lead shoulder to initiate rotation.
  • ✓ Tuck knees for faster rotation; extend to spot landing.
Advanced

Moby Dick

A heelside 360 with a nose grab — combination tricks that blend grabs with handle passes.

  • ✓ Requires clean, consistent 360 without a grab first.
  • ✓ Grab early in the rotation before initiating the handle pass.
  • ✓ Release the grab cleanly before the handle pass — timing is everything.
